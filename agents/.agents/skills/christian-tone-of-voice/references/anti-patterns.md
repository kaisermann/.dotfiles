# Anti-patterns

Use this as the cleanup pass after the voice is set. The goal is to remove generic AI writing signals without flattening Christian's actual tone.

## Filler and throat-clearing

Cut openers and transitions that delay the point:

- `here's the thing`
- `here's what`
- `here's why`
- `let me be clear`
- `it turns out`
- `it's worth noting`
- `in this section`
- `to sum up`
- `in conclusion`

Cut emphasis crutches and teacher-mode phrasing unless the audience truly needs it:

- `let that sink in`
- `full stop`
- `make no mistake`
- `let's break this down`
- `let's unpack`
- `think of it as`
- `imagine a world where`

## Formulaic structures

Avoid patterns that read as model-default prose:

- `not X, but Y`
- `not because X. Because Y.`
- `not a X. not a Y. a Z.`
- self-posed questions like `the result?` or `the worst part?`
- dramatic fragments like `Speed. That's it. That's the tradeoff.`
- fake-prose listicles like `the first... the second... the third...`
- repeated three-item rhythms and repeated sentence openings

Fix by stating the point directly.

## Specificity and agency

- Name the actor. Avoid false agency like `the decision emerged`, `the conversation moved`, `the culture shifted`, `the data tells us`.
- Replace vague claims like `the implications are significant` with the specific implication.
- Prefer one concrete example over broad abstraction or stacks of analogies.
- Avoid invented labels unless the term is real, established, and necessary.
- Avoid vague attributions like `experts argue` if you cannot name the source.

## Word choice

Prefer plain language over business or AI-favorite wording:

- `leverage` -> `use`
- `navigate` -> `handle`, `address`
- `landscape` -> `situation`, `field`
- `ecosystem` -> `system`, `community`
- `robust` -> `strong`, `solid`
- `framework` -> `approach`, `structure` when that is what you mean
- `delve`, `tapestry`, `nuanced` -> usually replace with something simpler

Watch for adverbs that try to manufacture importance:

- `quietly`
- `deeply`
- `fundamentally`
- `remarkably`
- `actually`
- `genuinely`
- `importantly`
- `crucially`

Cut them unless they do real semantic work.

## Rhythm and formatting

- Vary sentence lengths.
- Do not end every paragraph with a punchline.
- Do not repeat one metaphor across the whole piece.
- No em dashes.
- Avoid bold-first bullet formatting.
- Avoid signposted conclusions and fractal summaries.

## Trust the reader

- State the point instead of announcing that a point is coming.
- Do not pad one idea into many paragraphs with restatements.
- Do not inflate stakes to sound profound.
- Do not add fake vulnerability, fake suspense, or a detached narrator voice.

## Fast checklist

- Any throat-clearing before the point?
- Any `not X, but Y` or rhetorical-question pattern?
- Any vague claim that should become concrete?
- Any inanimate thing doing a human action?
- Any jargon, adverb, or AI-favorite wording that can be cut?
- Any paragraph ending with a manufactured punchline?
- Any repeated point, metaphor, or three-item pattern?
- Does the final sentence clarify scope, boundary, or next step?
